What Is VA Residual Income?

Residual income is the cash you have left every month after taxes, your full mortgage payment, and your major debts are subtracted. The VA uses this number as the real approval gate on a VA loan — not debt-to-income ratio like conventional and FHA loans. Most online "VA affordability" calculators completely skip this test, which is why so many Veterans get pre-approved on paper, then denied at underwriting.

Why DTI Alone Isn't Enough

Conventional and FHA loans approve based primarily on debt-to-income ratio — a percentage of your gross income. The VA realized decades ago this is a flawed measure: a 40% DTI in California means something very different than a 40% DTI in Mississippi, because cost of living varies dramatically.

So the VA added the residual income test on top of DTI. After everything is paid — taxes, mortgage, debts, even an allowance for utilities and maintenance — do you have enough actual dollars left to feed your family, fuel your car, and live a normal life? That's residual income. It's a dollar-amount test, not a percentage test, and it changes by region because $1,000 means different things in different places.

The 20% Cushion Rule

Here's a key compensating factor most Veterans don't know about: if your debt-to-income ratio is over 41% (which is the soft cap on VA loans), you can still qualify if your residual income exceeds the regional minimum by at least 20%. This is the most common save for borrowers who would otherwise fail DTI.

Example: A family of 4 in the Midwest needs $1,003 minimum residual income. If their DTI is 45% but their residual income is $1,250 or higher (1.20 × $1,003), the loan can still get approved.

What Goes Into the Calculation

VA underwriters subtract the following from your gross income to find residual:

Federal and state income taxes. Calculated based on your gross taxable income. This calculator uses a simplified estimate; actual underwriting uses your pay stubs and tax returns.

FICA (Social Security + Medicare). 7.65% of earned income, capped at the Social Security wage base.

Your full proposed mortgage payment (PITI). Principal, interest, property taxes, and homeowners insurance. HOA dues count if applicable.

All monthly debt payments. Minimum payments on auto loans, credit cards, student loans, alimony, child support, and anything else showing on your credit report.

An estimated utility and maintenance allowance. VA uses approximately $0.14 per square foot per month for utilities. This calculator estimates utilities and maintenance based on home price.

Whatever's left is your residual income. Compare it to the chart minimum for your family size and region.

What If You Don't Pass?

Failing residual income doesn't automatically kill your loan. Per VA Pamphlet 26-7, lenders are instructed to review residual income "in conjunction with all other credit factors." Compensating factors that can help:

Excellent credit history. Conservative credit use. Minimal consumer debt. Long-term employment. Significant liquid assets. Sizable down payment (yes, even on a VA loan). Equity from refinancing. Little or no increase in shelter expenses vs your current rent. Military benefits. Successful homeownership history.

That said, marginal residual income paired with anything else weak in the file (high DTI, mediocre credit, short employment history) usually results in a decline. The cleaner the rest of your file, the more residual income flexibility you have.

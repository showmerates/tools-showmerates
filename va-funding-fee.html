How the VA Funding Fee Works (2026)

The VA funding fee is a one-time charge paid to the Department of Veterans Affairs. It helps fund the VA loan program so it can keep operating at $0 down with no PMI. You can either pay it at closing or finance it into your loan.

Who's Exempt From the VA Funding Fee?

If you qualify for an exemption, the funding fee is $0 — which can save thousands of dollars at closing.

You're exempt if any of these apply:

  • You receive VA compensation for a service-connected disability
  • You're eligible to receive VA disability compensation but receive retirement pay instead
  • You're a Purple Heart recipient currently serving on active duty
  • You're a surviving spouse of a Veteran who died in service or from a service-connected disability
  • You're a surviving spouse currently receiving Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC)
  • You're a service member who has received a proposed or memorandum rating before the loan closing date

Note: If you're entitled to a disability rating but it hasn't been finalized at closing, you may still qualify and could be eligible for a refund of any funding fee already paid.

Should I Pay the Funding Fee at Closing or Finance It?

Either option is allowed. Financing the fee into the loan increases your loan balance and total interest paid over time, but reduces the cash you need at closing. For most Veterans with limited cash reserves, financing makes sense; for Veterans with extra cash who plan to stay in the home long-term, paying at closing saves money on interest. A licensed VA loan specialist can walk you through both scenarios with your real numbers.

First-Use vs Subsequent-Use: Why It Matters

Your first VA loan ever carries the lower 2.15% rate (with $0 down). After that, "subsequent use" jumps to 3.30%. On a $400,000 loan, that's a $4,600 difference. There are some exceptions — for example, if your only prior use was for a manufactured home, you may still qualify for the first-use rate. A specialist can verify your status through the VA's Certificate of Eligibility (COE) system before you close.
